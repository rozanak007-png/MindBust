Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 1, 2025  |  Effective Date: January 1, 2025

🔒 Your privacy matters. MindBurst does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third parties. This policy explains clearly what data we collect and exactly how it is used.

1. Who We Are

MindBurst ("we", "our", "us", "the website") is a free mental wellness platform offering interactive tools including a PHQ-9 mood assessment quiz, a spin wheel of mood-boosting activities, science-backed daily tips, and a mood tracker.

Our website is accessible at: https://yourusername.github.io/mindburst/

For any privacy-related questions, contact us at: your@email.com

2. Information We Collect

a) Information You Provide Voluntarily:

  • Responses to our PHQ-9 depression/mood assessment quiz (processed locally in your browser — not transmitted to our servers)
  • Mood tracker entries you log during your session (stored only in your browser session memory)
  • Your name, email address, and message if you submit our contact form

b) Information Collected Automatically When You Visit:

  • IP address and approximate geographic location (country and city level)
  • Browser type, version, and operating system
  • Pages visited, time spent on each page, and navigation path
  • Device type (mobile, desktop, or tablet)
  • Referring website or search engine that brought you to us
  • Date and time of your visit

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide, maintain, and improve our mental wellness tools and website content
  • To understand how visitors use our website through anonymous analytics
  • To display relevant advertisements through Google AdSense
  • To respond to contact form messages and enquiries
  • To detect, prevent, and address technical issues or abusive behavior
  • To comply with legal obligations

4. Google AdSense and Advertising

We use Google AdSense to display third-party advertisements on our website. Google AdSense uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our website and other sites across the internet.

Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your browsing history. You may opt out of personalized advertising at any time by visiting: Google Ads Settings.

For more information about how Google uses data when you use our site, please visit: How Google uses data from partner sites.

Third-party advertising partners may also use cookies. We have no control over the cookies used by third-party advertisers.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your web browser. We use the following types of cookies:

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for the website to function properly. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Used by Google Analytics to collect anonymous information about how visitors interact with our site. This helps us improve the user experience.
  • Advertising cookies: Used by Google AdSense to display advertisements relevant to you. These are set by Google and its advertising partners.
  • Preference cookies: Remember your settings and preferences on our site.

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of some features on our website.

6. Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to analyze website traffic and understand visitor behavior in aggregate, anonymous form. Google Analytics collects data such as pages visited, time on site, and general geographic location — but does not identify individual users.

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

7. Data Storage and Security

Health data: Quiz responses and mood tracker entries are processed entirely within your browser (client-side) and are never transmitted to or stored on our servers. We do not maintain any database of your personal health information.

Contact data: If you submit our contact form, your name and email address are stored securely and used only to respond to your message. We do not add you to any marketing lists without your explicit consent.

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect any data we collect. However, no internet transmission or electronic storage method is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Third-Party Links and Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites such as crisis helplines, mental health resources, and external tools. These third-party sites have their own privacy policies, which we encourage you to review. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of any linked third-party websites.

9. Children's Privacy

MindBurst is not directed at, and is not intended for use by, children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided personal information to us, please contact us immediately at your@email.com and we will take steps to delete that information.

10. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate personal data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to Object: Object to the processing of your personal data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for data processing at any time

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at your@email.com. We will respond to all verified requests within 30 days.

11. GDPR Compliance (European Union Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Our legal bases for processing your data include:

  • Legitimate interests: Website analytics and security monitoring
  • Consent: Advertising cookies (you may withdraw consent at any time)
  • Contract performance: Responding to your contact form submissions

12. CCPA Compliance (California Residents)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • The right to know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, or sell
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information
  • The right to opt out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell personal information)
  • The right to non-discrimination for exercising your CCPA rights

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for legal, operational, or regulatory reasons. When we make significant changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

Your continued use of MindBurst after any changes to this Privacy Policy constitutes your acceptance of the updated terms.
